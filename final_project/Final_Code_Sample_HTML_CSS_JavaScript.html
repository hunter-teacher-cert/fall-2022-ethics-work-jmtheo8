Digital Accessibility





Is defined as the inclusive practice of removing barriers that prevent interaction with, or access to websites, digital tools and technologies, by people with disabilities.


ACCESSIBLE SITES are ethical and allow easein FINDING, NAVIGATING and UNDERSTANDING information.


ACCESSIBLE SITES also provided clarity and consistency and equity for users of all abilities.


ETHICAL DIGITAL ACCESSIBILITY ALLOWS: Equivalent accessibility to information, interactions, services and ease of use experiences on digital platforms for all.

“Of the 5.4 billion searches conducted via Google’s search engine, only 2% of all websites are accessible. Ethical digital accessiblity allows for equivalent accessibility to information, interactions, services and ease of use experiences on digital platforms for all."


THE AMERICAN DISAILITY ACT OF 1990:
Is a civil rights law that prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in all areas of public life, including jobs, schools, transportation, and all public and private places that are open to the general public. The purpose of the law is to make sure that people with disabilities have the same rights and opportunities as everyone else.



TYPES OF DISABILITIES ADDRESSED BY WEB ACCESSIBLE SITES:

  • sensorineural
  • conductive
  • mixed hearing loss
  • color blind
  • low vision
  • no vision
  • stuttering
  • apraxia
  • dysarthria
  • fine motor control
  • keyboard/switch only
  • slow response time

GOOD PRACTICES

“Approximately 61 million people in the US have a disability."


EXAMPLES OF DESIGN STRATEGIES:

  • Use proper content management systems (CMS)
    That supports accessibility like WordPress, along with an accessible theme/template, modules, plugins, and widgets.

  • Ensuring you add Alt text to all images in the code.
    Complex ideas should have more extensive descriptions near the image in the form of a caption or descriptive summaries.

  • Web content should be keyboard-accessible
    Many users with mobility disabilities like repetitive stress injuries can’t access the content with a mouse or trackpad.

  • Make the proper use of colors
    With 8% of the population suffering from red-green color deficiency, using only these colors to indicate form fields prevent them from understanding your message

  • Use large buttons, controls, and links
    Make it a point not to place large buttons, links, and controls too close to each other. Doing this promotes easier usability in users with minor impairments.

  • Conducting a section 508 test
    To ensure you have compliant websites and resources by running your websites link through sites like https://wave.webaim.org.

  • Obstacles to access and ethical dilemas develop for users with disabilities
    Website made compliant with WCAG standards will always win on the SEO front because a lot of web accessibility practice involves the use of semantic code which search engines find easy to crawl over and index.

  • Potential for exposure to lawsuit.
    There is a present notable growing trend in businesses getting sued for websites that lack essential web accessible features. Inclusion of WCAG, Web Content Accessibility Guidelines recommendations to websites opens the door for better relationships and a decrease in exposure to ethical dilemmas that surface when web accessibility features are not included on websites.

  • Loss of revenue.
    Research undertaken by UserWay, a leader in website accessibility automation, has identified that e-commerce retailers stand to lose an estimated $828 million in revenue during the 2021 Christmas holiday as a result of poor website accessibility.
